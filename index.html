📖 Source Guide — How to Read War Intelligence
WIRE Reuters — Baseline Gold Standard
Primary wire service — most outlets rewrite Reuters. Extremely high accuracy for hard facts (missile strikes, coordinates, timestamps). Weakness: Slow on context — they wait for absolute verification. Best for the "current price" of news.
→ Reuters World News RSS
CONTEXT Al Jazeera — Counter-Narrative Perspective
Qatar-based · Granular coverage of Iranian internal sentiment and the Axis of Resistance (Hezbollah, Houthis) vs Western outlets. Use for: Hormuz supply risk, Iranian-side threats, local supply chain disruptions. Watch for: Pro-regional framing — use harder language for US/Israeli actions, softer for Iranian-aligned. → Al Jazeera Middle East
SENTIMENT Google News — Retail/Algorithmic Mood
Not a news service — it's an algorithm that reflects Western market sentiment. If flooded with "Escalation" headlines → XLE and oil react regardless of physical accuracy. Use for: Setting your War Score and Market Sentiment fields. It tells you what the herd is thinking. → Google News: Iran Oil Hormuz
📊 Institutional Layer — Smart Money Sources

These firms define the market baseline. If BlackRock says "Tactically Overweight Energy" due to supply shock → every Iran headline gets 2× volatility. → BlackRock Investment Institute
FEAR S&P Global Platts · Energy Aspects — Volatility Layer
Industry-specific price assessments. If they mention "Dated Brent premiums rising" or "Hormuz insurance surcharges" → sentiment is officially Bullish/Panic. Gem logic: Search "Platts energy market alert" or "Energy Aspects Iran update."
MOMENTUM Bloomberg · Investing.com — Retail / Algo Layer
Where the War Score truly lives. HFTs use Bloomberg headlines to trigger buy/sell orders. If headlines say "Iran Ceasefire Fragile" → algorithms sell even if physical oil hasn't moved. Gem logic: Use Google News to aggregate 24-hour tone as your quick-check.

Exit Framework
Thesis trade: exit based on why you hold, not just on price level
TIER 1 — THESIS EXITS  ·  PRIMARY
🎯 War score drops below 40 — de-escalation confirmed (not rumoured) → reduce all energy exposure immediately
🛢️ Brent closes below $82 for 2 consecutive days — back to early-March entry levels, war premium fully unwound, thesis over
✍️ Ceasefire formally signed — not talks, not rumours. Confirmed ceasefire → EXIT FRO immediately, reduce KAR/OXY by 50%
📊 Conviction drops below 55% — emergency de-risk: sell KAR → 500 shares, ALL FRO, 50% OXY. Hold DRO (uncorrelated)
TIER 2 — CAPITAL PROTECTION FLOOR  ·  ABSOLUTE BACKSTOP
5% below avg entry — ratcheted up from 15% because all positions are in profit. Since you're already up 10%+ on all four stocks, a 15% floor lets you lose all paper gains and more. The 5% floor means your worst case is a small loss from entry, not giving back everything you've made. Never move this wider.

TIER 3 — PROFIT-TRAILING STOP (EMA-ATR)  ·  AFTER PROFIT-TAKING ONLY · WIDEN AS YOU SELL
Only activate once substantial profit has been taken. The less you have left to protect, the wider you should set the trailing stop — you want to let house money run as far as possible without getting shaken out by noise.
Full position held
No trailing stop — thesis exits only
After tranche 1–2 (~20% sold)
EMA-ATR × 2.0 — some profit banked, still significant exposure
After tranche 3 (~40% sold)
EMA-ATR × 2.5 — most profits locked, loosen up
House money remainder
EMA-ATR × 3.0 — pure profit, let it run far
Replaces the old "8–10% at +10%" and "5% at +25%" rules — EMA-ATR does the same job using real measured volatility.
